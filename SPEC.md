# Bot Conquest — Game Specification

## World vision

Bot Conquest takes place across one interconnected mechanical world. It is not divided into numbered sectors, discrete stages, or finish-line courses. Its spaces support exploration, backtracking, alternate routes, optional rooms, shortcuts, hidden caches, environmental storytelling, and small secrets.

The current replacement fragment spans 14,500 horizontal units and 3,700 vertical units. It is not the complete map and has no region-clear state, glowing finish gate, or traversal-percentage objective.

## Player

The player controls a compact spherical/oval mechanical bot. Its body collision box is 50 × 36 world units, making it visibly wider than tall. Three red optical sensors communicate facing direction.

Three mechanical legs connect body-mounted anchors to actual nearby platform surfaces. Their targets are spread laterally by 44 units with up to 112 units of reach so they remain visible around the body. The center foot receives a left/right bias instead of hiding directly beneath the bot. A foot remains latched during each step interval, then one leg at a time selects a slightly randomized reachable contact point. If no surface exists within leg range, that leg retracts and is not rendered.

### Abilities

| Action | Input | Required behavior |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Move / horizontal aim | `A`, `D` | Accelerate left/right and aim in that direction |
| Jump / aim up | `W` | Perform the starting basic jump and set upward aim |
| Double jump | `W` in air | Locked at the beginning; available after its future pickup |
| Wall movement | Hold/press `W` at wall | Locked at the beginning; available after its future pickup |
| Vault | Hold `W` while running into a low obstacle | Locked initially; after the post-boss pickup, hop onto obstacles up to 115 units high |
| Aim down | `S` | Set downward aim without moving |
| Dash | `Shift` | Locked at the beginning; the Rift Stalker core grants the short horizontal burst |
| Basic slash | `Space` | Instantly strike the full 105-unit area in the locked aim direction |
| Repair | `E` | Spend 30 electricity, then channel for 0.7 seconds to restore one missing shell; damage interrupts it, attacks are locked out during the channel, and it cannot start during post-hit invulnerability |
| Electric field | `Q` | Locked initially; the Crown Dynamo's Field Core grants it and supplies one 40-electricity charge |
| Electric jab | `F` | Locked initially; spend 24 electricity after collecting the blue Volt Jab core |
| Interact | `O` | Rest, enter/leave an unlocked merchant room, open a merchant catalog, or buy its selected offer |
| Inventory / map | `I` | Toggle a simulation-pausing overlay; use `A`/`D` for pages, or use `Q` and `WASD` to zoom and select local maps |

There is no restart key or persistent restart control. Losing the final shell rebuilds the bot at the last activated save point, or at the initial spawn before any station has been activated.

The basic jump is grounded: running or walking beyond a platform edge consumes it, so it cannot be delayed until the bot is already airborne. After double jump is unlocked, leaving an edge without jumping preserves exactly one airborne jump, the same allowance that remains after a normal grounded jump.

The basic slash supports left, right, up, and down. Its aim is captured when the attack begins. Pressing Space immediately evaluates the complete 105-unit directional hitbox once. Its only presentation is a 0.09-second straight white slash facing forward; it never extends, retracts, curves, or resembles a pointer.

### Ability pickups

Movement and combat upgrades use generic contact pickups. An available pickup is a small floating square with a bright ability-specific color, orbiting lines, motes, and a gentle bob. Moving the bot into it collects it immediately and permanently marks it collected for the run. Collection opens a large temporary integration popup showing the ability name, its input, and a short usage instruction.

The blue `volt-core` lies inside the sealed Abyss Warden reward chamber in the Sunken Vault. It is unavailable until the full boss dies, then unlocks Volt Jab and shows `F` in its integration popup. A 24-electricity conduit and a two-health powered seal follow on the escape route, teaching the player to harvest the conduit with basic slashes and spend one Volt Jab to continue.

The white `vault-core` just beyond the Heavy Core does not appear until that boss is defeated. Collecting it unlocks wall climb. Holding `W` against a wall climbs continuously; pressing `D` away from a left wall or `A` away from a right wall jumps from the current height. The ability never teleports the bot to the top. Future pickups use the same data and collection flow with their own progression requirements.

The cyan `field-core` appears only after the Crown Dynamo is defeated. It unlocks the circular Field attack and raises the bot to at least 40 electricity so its first use is immediately available. That supplied charge opens the nearby `crown-field-seal`, teaching the ability before the newly accessible Crownworks annex.

### Shell bodies

The standard shell starts with three maximum core shells, 100 electricity capacity, 105 Fusion Cutter range, and normal shell, core, legs, and Fusion Cutter mounts.

The first alternate shell is the Slicer. It waits in a contained west pocket of the Crownworks Field annex behind a second wall that only Volt Jab can break. Its body is a forward-pointed droplet with three red circular eyes. The Slicer trades one maximum core shell and 20 electricity capacity for 30 additional base cutter range. It has no shell mount; instead its normal mounts are core, legs, Fusion Cutter, and a second weapon position named Extended Fusion Cutter.

Once the Slicer is owned, resting opens a large shell-selection menu and pauses simulation. Arrow keys choose an owned shell, `O` equips it, and `I` closes the selector without switching. Every shell stores its own modifier and relic placements. Switching away saves that build, while switching back restores it exactly. Future shells must continue changing both silhouette and statistics rather than acting as recolors; a tall/light jump shell and large/heavy durability shell remain later design candidates.

### Body modifications

The standard body has four full-strength normal mounts: shell, core, legs, and the Fusion Cutter. The Slicer has core, legs, Fusion Cutter, and Extended Fusion Cutter instead. A purchased modifier is reusable and occupies at most one mount on the active shell. The ITEMS page shows a current-shell diagram, every live mount, acquired-item icons, and a tooltip for the selected item. Pressing `Q` on a modifier enters placement mode; the arrow keys move through only that modifier's compatible mounts and storage, and pressing `Q` again places it in the highlighted target. If the destination is occupied, its previous item returns to storage. Modifiers are deliberately not universal: Aegis Filament and Reactive Governor cannot be installed on either cutter mount.

Each modifier changes meaning by placement rather than repeating one ascending stat profile. Aegis Filament costs 650 scrap: shell adds exactly one maximum shell, core adds 10 electricity capacity, legs add 18 movement speed, and an internal installation provides a one-hit enemy-contact guard only while a repair channel is active. Reactive Governor costs 800 scrap: shell adds 0.35 seconds to the post-hit window, core returns 8 electricity after taking damage, legs grant 85 movement speed for 3 seconds after damage, and internal placement grants a reduced 30 speed for 2 seconds. Extender Arm costs 950 scrap: core adds 8 capacity, legs add 30 movement speed, the Fusion Cutter gains 40 range, and internal placement gives only 12 cutter range. The only modifier that increases health is Aegis Filament on the shell mount, and it adds one shell.

Every shell starts with exactly one unlocked Internal Bay. The Inner Foundry does not sell or unlock it; the merchant sells only Auxiliary Bay A and Auxiliary Bay B for 1,600 and 2,800 scrap. The auxiliary bays are not classified or presented as internal. Every purchased auxiliary expansion is installed on every owned shell, and all three expansion positions use each modifier's intentionally reduced internal behavior instead of a generic mixed-stat penalty. Each body owns its normal mount array and saved loadout, so future bodies can expose different parts and additional placements without changing the inventory system.

### Relics

Relics are permanent merchant items and the single name for this item category. They have no dedicated relic slots. A purchased relic remains inactive in storage until installed in any normal mount exposed by the current shell. Relics share those mounts with modifiers, displace or are displaced by any occupying item, and retain the same effect regardless of which normal mount holds them. The Slicer's Extended Fusion Cutter is a valid normal mount; relics still cannot use the internal or auxiliary expansion bays.

Eight distinct relics are distributed across five merchants and reinforce each specialist's core service. Shell Archive sells Mender Loop (750 scrap, repairs cost 5 less electricity) and Impact Damper (900, half knockback after damage) beside its maximum-shell tiers. Capacitor Exchange sells Feedback Dynamo (850, taking damage restores 12 electricity) and Execution Coil (1,050, enemy kills restore 8 electricity) beside its capacity tiers. Edge Forge sells Kinetic Memory (1,250, the next Fusion Cutter slash after damage deals +2) beside its damage tiers. The hidden Response Forge sells Arc Retort (1,100, taking damage deals 2 damage to enemies within 120 units), while the hidden Salvage Curator sells Salvage Lens (800, ordinary enemies award +3 scrap) and Corpse Key (950, recovery wrecks have one health). These effects are conditional and combinable rather than ascending copies of one stat.

## Resource loop

The starting primary slash deals three damage. Crawlers and rollers have six health, hoppers and drones have nine, and the larger brute has twelve, producing two-hit, three-hit, and four-hit starting encounters. Enemies award scrap when killed: crawler 6, roller 8, hopper 10, drone 12, and brute 25. The Grand Exchange Edge Forge offers four increasingly expensive, run-persistent `+1` primary-slash upgrades at 500, 900, 1,500, and 2,400 scrap plus the cutter-focused Kinetic Memory relic. Mk 1 needs scrap only; Mk 2 also requires one titanium, Mk 3 two titanium and one uranium, and Mk 4 three titanium and two uranium. Each increase remains incremental. Purchased edge coils appear in the inventory and a completed forge cannot be purchased again.

Titanium and uranium are persistent special materials used by later Edge Forge tiers. Their world density is intentionally low: two ordinary single-material salvage piles remain hidden on ability-gated upper routes, the Cache Scrapper awards three titanium, and the Shard Gauntlet summit cache awards 450 scrap, three titanium, and two uranium.

The player starts with an electricity capacity of 100. A standard slash against an enemy adds 12 electricity up to the current capacity. The Capacitor Exchange sells three persistent `+25` capacity tiers for 400, 800, and 1,400 scrap. Modifier capacity bonuses are deliberately small: core placements add at most 10, and no current internal placement adds capacity. Three conduits each contain 24 electricity, yield 4 per slash, and stay permanently empty after six successful harvests. A target hit by a special attack returns 4 electricity.

The Shell Archive sells three persistent `+1` maximum-shell tiers for 450, 900, and 1,600 scrap. Buying a shell or capacitor tier also fills the newly added capacity. Modifier placement changes maximum statistics without refilling them, and removing a modifier clamps the current value to the new maximum.

Ordinary junk piles have configured health and burst into scrap or special-material payouts when destroyed. Two powered seals have minimum damage 2 and explicitly require electric jab: the two-health Sunken Vault tutorial seal and the two-health wall protecting the Slicer in the Field annex. A later armored cache wall also has minimum damage 2. Crownworks additionally has a one-health Field-only seal that ignores the basic slash and Volt Jab. Hitting junk does not generate electricity.

The electric field lasts 0.9 seconds and uses a true 112-unit-radius circular collision test. The electric jab lasts 0.5 seconds, reaches 170 units in its locked direction, and deals two damage.

## Lives and failure

The player starts with three core shells. Enemy contact removes one shell and briefly grants invulnerability. A repair guard from an internally mounted Aegis Filament absorbs one enemy hit without losing a shell or interrupting that active repair channel. It disappears when used or when repair completes or is cancelled, never remains active afterward, and does not negate spikes or falling. Touching a spike immediately removes one shell and returns the player to the last safe position recorded on the most recently supported platform; the player never continues falling through the spike pit. Falling completely out of the world still returns to the initial spawn. Losing the final shell rebuilds the bot with its current maximum shells at its last activated save point. Carried electricity and scrap drop to zero, while titanium, uranium, upgrades, modifiers, and special items persist. A three-health wreck remains at the death location holding the lost scrap. Destroying that wreck returns all stored scrap but no electricity. A newer death replaces any unrecovered wreck. Full death reconstructs all ordinary enemies; defeated full bosses and mini bosses stay defeated.

## Enemies

Selected crawlers and rollers patrol passively within configured leashes. Basic ground enemies detect at 210 units and brutes at 240 units. Distance alone is insufficient: both the player and ground enemy must stand on the same supporting platform before pursuit begins. Ground enemies probe for walkable support before every step. Main-route ground encounters occupy usable combat floor rather than narrow pockets caught between a raised block and spikes.

Crawlers change direction immediately and pursue at a steady 75 units per second. Oblong rollers accelerate gradually toward a 150-unit-per-second chase speed, preserving momentum rather than copying crawler motion. Square brutes stop for a visible 0.38-second warning, lock a direction, and charge at 190 units per second for 0.42 seconds before cooling down. Hoppers commit to a high, fast leap toward the player's horizontal position, then remain grounded for 0.7 seconds after landing so the player has a reliable attack window.

Drones detect at 340 units. When inactive they hover around their spawn height. Once active they steer toward the player's actual two-dimensional position at 105 units per second. Drone movement resolves horizontal and vertical collision against solid blocks, so drones cannot fly through floors, ceilings, or walls.

Current archetypes are crawler, roller, hopper, drone, and brute. Their silhouettes use hard industrial chassis, pistons, claws, spikes, and needle-like feet rather than soft creature shapes. Crawlers and rollers take two unmodified slashes, hoppers and drones take three, and brutes take four; bosses and mini bosses retain their larger explicit health pools.

Every boss and mini boss crosses into phase two exactly once at half health. The transition never supplies a damage shield or emits particles, and the existing attack state continues uninterrupted; it never resets an attack timer or prevents incoming damage. Each full boss gains a fourth overdrive-only attack. Full-boss attacks are chosen from a seeded pseudo-random pool with no immediate repeats instead of following a fixed sequence.

### Boss encounter

After traversing roughly two-thirds of the current route, the player enters a 1,300-unit-wide open arena. Crossing its inner trigger while physically inside the chamber drops a solid gate behind the player and another at the far exit. The gates finish closing in about 0.3 seconds and cannot be crossed while the boss remains alive. Full-height structural bulkheads seal both approaches to the arena roof, preventing roof bypasses and ensuring a gate can never lock the player outside the fight.

The Heavy Core boss has 72 health and randomly chooses its charge, aerial slam, or projectile volley without immediate repeats. Its 96-by-78-unit collision body remains imposing but can be cleared cleanly with the starting jump, preventing its charge from creating an unavoidable wall pin. At half health it enters phase two with faster recovery, a 340-unit-per-second charge, a larger slam shockwave, a three-bolt volley, and a four-bolt radial burst.

- A telegraphed horizontal charge locks its direction before rushing at 260 units per second.
- An aerial slam launches upward, then creates an expanding 300-unit ground shockwave when it lands.
- A volley fires two energy bolts toward the player's current position with a small angular spread.

The HUD displays the boss health bar throughout the encounter. Defeating the Heavy Core awards 150 scrap, removes remaining boss hazards, retracts both gates, destroys both full-height red roof bulkheads, and permanently clears the arena for the run.

### Mini-boss encounter

The Sunken Vault contains the sixty-health Abyss Warden, a full boss with its own large health bar and seeded-random charge, leaping shockwave, and three-bolt volley selection. Its chamber is 830 units wide and 320 units tall, providing broad charge runway and enough vertical clearance for the leap to read cleanly. Its 420-unit-tall right exit seal is solid before the encounter activates, reaches from Y 700 to the floor at Y 1120, and cannot be jumped around from the exit platforms. Dropping into the chamber activates the Warden and closes the entry seal. The dormant Warden cannot be damaged from the approach. No left-side hiding shelf sits above the arena or obscures the later deep-route hatch. At half health phase two accelerates its movement and recovery, enlarges the shockwave, expands the volley to four bolts, and adds a six-bolt radial crossfire.

Defeating the Abyss Warden awards 110 scrap, removes its hazards, opens both sides, releases the blue Volt Jab core inside the chamber, and opens the floor hatch toward Dash on that same damage frame. The opened escape leads to the tutorial conduit and powered seal.

Quiet Drift contains the reusable thirty-health Cache Scrapper mini-boss on an optional upper route. At half health it enters a faster, longer-charge phase two. It awards three titanium; the normal foundation route remains open and it never protects an ability.

### Rift Stalker

The instant the Abyss Warden is defeated, its floor opens into a clearly readable hatch without waiting for Volt Jab, Wall Climb, another location check, or a later simulation frame. The drop lands safely on a broad entry platform. Three broad steps climb the right side back to the Warden exit with the starting jump, so discovering the hatch early never traps the player. The contained route onward still requires Wall Climb: the first landing leads over a 200-unit west climb into its gallery, then a controlled drop leads to a 220-unit east climb and second gallery. Structural roof and side walls remove outside falls, stair-to-gallery skips, and side-drop shortcuts before two further controlled drops descend to the Rift Stalker floor at Y 2,550.

The ninety-six-health Rift Stalker randomly chooses three attacks without immediate repeats: a fast dash across the chamber, a relocation above the player followed by a stationary half-second warning hover and then a vertical ground slam, or one 520-unit-per-second projectile that tracks briefly before committing to its line. The projectile is meant to be dodged vertically and disappears without exploding when it touches the player, a solid platform, or the arena gate. Phase two raises the dash to 680 units per second, shortens the hover, enlarges the shockwave, fires two faster tracking bolts, and adds a six-bolt non-tracking rift burst that also never explodes. Defeating the boss awards 180 scrap, opens the exit, releases the Dash core, and raises a return route. Its horizontal spans require Dash, while its final two climb walls have full bot-width crest channels and physically reconnect to the Warden floor.

### Crown Dynamo

Wall Climb leads into a Crownworks chamber built around a central shaft and two separated floor masses. Entering either side closes two horizontal gate pieces across the shaft. The seventy-two-health Crown Dynamo alternates between two high anchor positions and remains damageable throughout every active state, including relocation, hovering, windup, attack, and recovery. Its changing white, yellow, and cyan colors communicate timing only and never indicate armor or invulnerability. The player still has to climb the chamber perches and attack upward instead of fighting entirely from the floor. Phase two shortens its hover and recovery windows, combines sweep and column hazards, and expands its volley to six bolts.

It randomly chooses a cross-floor sweep, targeted vertical column, or four-bolt volley without immediate repeats. The sweep has a full-width dashed horizontal warning matching the column's dashed vertical warning, and the Dynamo now spawns directly on both perch surfaces instead of hovering too high above them. Overdrive adds a two-column grid attack. Defeating it awards 140 scrap, clears its hazards, reopens the shaft, and releases the charged Field Core. Field then breaks the west seal into a substantial enclosed annex containing two traversable height layers, ordinary combat, salvage, and a reversible return. Its red platform braces hang overhead with a full-height passage beneath them, leaving the lower corridor clear until the second two-health wall; that wall requires Volt Jab and reveals the Slicer shell when destroyed.

### Recovery room

The foundation immediately beyond the boss arena is a calm recovery room with no ordinary enemies, junk, conduits, or floor obstacles. Its station powers on only after the Heavy Core is defeated. While within 115 units, the HUD displays the `O // REST AND RECOVER` prompt. Resting restores the current maximum core shells, clears current knockback, plays a recovery pulse, moves both the spike-reset checkpoint and full-death respawn point beside the station, and reconstructs all ordinary enemies. It does not refill electricity, revive defeated bosses or mini bosses, or unlock abilities. Once multiple shells are owned, the interaction then opens the paused shell selector; choosing another shell equips it at full current integrity while keeping electricity clamped to that body's capacity.

## Inventory and map

Pressing `I` opens the combined overlay and pauses world simulation. It starts on STATUS; outside the world overview, `A` and `D` move between MAP, STATUS, MATERIALS, and ITEMS while `W` and `S` move the selection within the active page. STATUS reports current/maximum shells, current/maximum electricity, Fusion Cutter damage and range, and scrap. MATERIALS reports only titanium and uranium. ITEMS lists merchant purchases with distinct icons, a tooltip for the selected entry, and a diagram of the current shell. The diagram shows that shell's actual normal mounts plus three expansion positions—one always-unlocked internal bay and two auxiliary bays—with only unpurchased auxiliary positions visibly locked. The standard shell includes a shell mount; the Slicer replaces it with Extended Fusion Cutter. It never shows relic-only slots. `Q` selects a modifier or relic for placement, every arrow key moves among compatible diagram targets, and `Q` confirms the highlighted mount or storage.

MAP sits to the left of STATUS and opens on the player's current region. A revealed local map diagrams that region's foundations, routes, hazards, merchant links, and current-player marker using one uniform world-to-map scale on both axes; it must never widen geometry to fill the panel. An unrevealed local map displays `NO MAP`. Pressing `Q` zooms out to a three-column overview of all eight regions. While zoomed out, `W`, `A`, `S`, and `D` select a region and never change inventory pages; pressing `Q` again zooms into the selected region. Three survey cores reveal configured west, central, and east groups that together cover the world.

## Current map fragment

The current map is a complete geometric reset and must not derive its layout from the discarded access-path prototype. It spans X 0–14,500 and Y -1,900–2,700. Seventeen foundation pieces form the lower traversal network. Within the Sunken Vault, five foundations descend from Y 680 to Y 820 and rise again in 70-unit increments. A reversible branch continues to Y 1,040 before one deliberate drop reaches the Abyss Warden floor at Y 1,120. The gate-controlled Warden escape uses three tested basic-jump platforms; no accidental early drop is allowed to strand the player. A Wall-Climb loft extends above the original Vault, Crownworks and its Field annex occupy the high world, and the post-Warden climb galleries and controlled descents extend to the Rift Stalker at Y 2,550.

Heavy overhead blocks frame large chambers. Thirty-two starting-kit platforms are scattered across at least nine of them as broad leaps, return drops, combat perches, and alternate crossings. They are not a staircase or serpentine chain. Most suspended platforms are 40–60 units thick; only the two deep Vault floor masses reach 80 units. Massive foundations remain at least 120 units thick except the five 60-unit Vault foundations, whose reduced depth leaves more passage clearance.

Fifty-five additional exploration surfaces make every named region substantially taller and denser without stretching the lower route into a linear sequence. Rusted Verge gains a substation loop and east watch; Sunken Vault gains flood shelves and Wall-Climb side lofts; Ember Foundry gains cooling, crane, and approach routes; Core Bastion gains a seven-platform maintenance tower revealed after the Heavy Core roof breaks; Crownworks gains lower galleries and extra upper-shaft ledges; Shard Gauntlet gains trapped service perches; Quiet Drift gains ceiling galleries; and Grand Exchange gains a multi-level archive route. Every region has at least six expansion surfaces, at least five ordinary enemies, platform hazards, and a new configured scrap cache.

The starting kit cannot reach the whole map. Twelve platforms form three substantial optional upper regions: upper assembly requires double jump, high foundry requires dash, and Crownworks requires wall movement. The Crownworks entrance uses a short 220-unit climb wall beside the upper relay platform, followed by an offset landing; it must never read or behave like the previous 540-unit red barrier. After Dash is collected, additional optional pockets in Rusted Verge, Ember Foundry, Shard Gauntlet, and Grand Exchange turn ordinary-region backtracking into new traversal and salvage opportunities without changing the tested lower route.

Eight named regions cover the current world and meet at seven visible mechanical gates. Core Bastion now spans both the Heavy Core chamber and the former Relay Concourse space from X 5,890–8,400; crossing the post-boss recovery room no longer creates a second region title or map page. The Shard Gauntlet combines three lower spike gaps, three upper-platform spike beds, two cycling electric barriers, and a swinging chain ball beneath its summit cache; Quiet Drift is a more conventional exploration chamber with an optional scrap mini-boss; the 1,800-unit Grand Exchange is a broad multi-height district with a spike-free route across the eastern market platform into the Edge Forge interaction pocket in its far corner. Region gates remain non-blocking navigation thresholds rather than stage exits.

The major upgrade merchants must be discovered beyond the immediate post-boss recovery route. Parts Broker occupies the Wall-Climb-gated Crownworks route, Capacitor Exchange sits on the Shard Gauntlet overlook, and Shell Archive is on a high Grand Exchange route. Additional doors remain in the Rusted Verge, Ember Foundry, and the Grand Exchange floor. Every doorway rectangle and its standing pocket remain outside solid platforms and walls. A door in a safe pocket can open immediately; otherwise it checks only a 210-unit circular field centered on that door. Ordinary enemies outside that small local field—including enemies directly above or below on inaccessible routes—cannot keep it sealed. Pressing `O` beneath an unlocked door moves the bot into a separate enemy-free merchant room, and its interior exit returns to the saved overworld position.

Pressing `O` near the merchant opens a large, simulation-pausing catalog rather than immediately purchasing an item. The catalog displays every offer in that shop with its full effect, scrap and material cost, and available, unaffordable, prerequisite-locked, or owned state. Each specialist displays a short specialty description and stocks synergistic relics: Shell Archive supports survivability, Capacitor Exchange supports charge recovery, and Edge Forge supports cutter output. Unsupported mounts are omitted from an offer instead of implying that every modifier can be used offensively. `W` and `S` move through the list, `O` purchases the selected available offer, and `I` closes the catalog. The current services sell health tiers, electricity-capacity tiers, three reusable body modifiers, two auxiliary bays, eight normal-mount relics, and the Edge Forge's four slash upgrades. The internal bay is starting equipment and never appears as merchant stock. Response Forge and Salvage Curator remain additional relic discoveries.

Enemies, junk, and secrets occupy the upper networks, the deep vault, and the ability-gated regions—not only the lower left-to-right floor. The map contains one hundred four ordinary enemy spawns, thirty-two static spike beds or gaps, three dynamic Gauntlet hazards, three exhaustible conduits, thirty-four junk piles, two Volt-only seals, one Field-only seal, one optional mini-boss encounter, four full boss encounters, and two end alcoves. The Sunken Vault uses a darker blue-black background, Shard Gauntlet uses red hazard silhouettes, and Grand Exchange uses warm industrial accents.

Crownworks continues well above its original roof line. Wall Climb carries the player through a narrow structural opening into the Crown Dynamo's broad, enclosed upper works with a heavy roof, two floor masses, climbable combat perches, and a safe return through the same shaft. Field opens the west wall into a roughly 2,550-unit-wide two-chamber annex rather than ending the route at a few ceilings. Its eastern chamber contains the Volt-gated Slicer pocket; beyond that pocket, a continuous lower corridor crosses the former Heavy Core east bulkhead into a larger western hall with several platform heights, four additional ordinary encounters, platform spikes, and two substantial scrap caches. The bulkhead footprint gains its short floor-and-roof connector only when the Heavy Core dies, so it cannot bypass the earlier boss. Both upper spaces are playable exploration rather than decorative roof geometry.

The deep Sunken Vault hatch becomes physically open as soon as the Abyss Warden dies. Retracting the old boss-floor mass exposes a broad, safe drop to the first landing; the former `vault-high` shelf remains absent so the opening is obvious. A three-step stair on the right leads back to the Warden exit with the starting jump, while its separation from the west gallery preserves Wall Climb as the requirement for continuing deeper. Beyond it, two broad side galleries, two mandatory climb walls, two ordinary encounters, and three tested controlled drops form the route to the boss arena.

Solid rectangles never intersect: platform faces may touch but cannot overlap, bury one another, or contain a placed enemy, conduit, or junk pile. Every intended starting-kit connection has a simulated safe traversal or return drop. Ordinary junk always leaves at least one bot-width bypass, eliminating narrow obstacle pockets that can strand the player.

The world contains no permanent embedded area-name captions, directional labels, or text-box-like room names. Visual depth comes from three parallax structure layers, deep unlabeled recesses, and massive blocks with beveled top and side faces. True walls use red vertical bracing, dark center rails, repeated chevrons, and no green floor edge, so their collision role is immediately readable. The HUD shows coordinates, lives, electricity, scrap, the available starting kit, and only the temporary region-entry title.
